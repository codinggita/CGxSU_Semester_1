# Assignment: Graph Representations

## Overview

This assignment covers three fundamental graph representations: adjacency matrices, adjacency lists, and incidence matrices. Understanding these representations and their trade-offs is essential for implementing efficient graph algorithms.

---

## Problems

### Problem 1: Adjacency Matrix for Path Graph

Given the graph:

```
A — B — C
```

Edges: $\{A,B\}, \{B,C\}$

**Task:** Construct the adjacency matrix.

**Solution:**

For undirected graph with vertices $\{A, B, C\}$:

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}
  & A & B & C \\
A & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
B & 1 & 0 & 1 \\
C & 0 & 1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

Entry $A[i][j] = 1$ if edge $\{i,j\}$ exists, $0$ otherwise. Matrix is symmetric for undirected graphs. $\square$

### Problem 2: Adjacency List

Given the graph:

```
1        3
 \      /
   2 — 4
```

Edges: $(1,2), (2,4), (4,3)$

**Task:** Write the adjacency list.

**Solution:**

**Adjacency List:**
- $1: [2]$
- $2: [1, 4]$
- $3: [4]$
- $4: [2, 3]$

Each vertex maps to its list of neighbors. $\square$

### Problem 3: Incidence Matrix

Given the graph:

```
X — Y — Z
```

Edges: $e_1 = \{X,Y\}, e_2 = \{Y,Z\}$

**Task:** Construct the incidence matrix.

**Solution:**

$$I = \begin{bmatrix}
  & e_1 & e_2 \\
X &  1  &  0  \\
Y &  1  &  1  \\
Z &  0  &  1
\end{bmatrix}$$

Entry $I[v][e] = 1$ if vertex $v$ is incident to edge $e$. $\square$

### Problem 4: Adjacency Matrix for Directed Graph

Given the directed graph:

```
A → B → C
↑       ↓
└───────D
```

Edges: $(A,B), (B,C), (C,D), (D,A)$

**Task:** Write the adjacency matrix.

**Solution:**

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}
  & A & B & C & D \\
A & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
B & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
C & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\
D & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

Entry $A[i][j] = 1$ if directed edge $(i,j)$ exists. Matrix is NOT symmetric for directed graphs. $\square$

### Problem 5: Adjacency List for Triangle

Given the graph:

```
  P
 / \
Q - R
```

Edges: $\{P,Q\}, \{P,R\}, \{Q,R\}$

**Task:** Construct the adjacency list.

**Solution:**

- $P: [Q, R]$
- $Q: [P, R]$
- $R: [P, Q]$

This is the complete graph $K_3$. Each vertex has degree 2. $\square$

### Problem 6: Graph from Adjacency Matrix

Given adjacency matrix:

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}
  & A & B & C \\
A & 0 & 1 & 1 \\
B & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
C & 1 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

**Task:** Draw the graph.

**Solution:**

From the matrix:
- $A[A][B] = 1$: edge $\{A,B\}$ exists
- $A[A][C] = 1$: edge $\{A,C\}$ exists
- $A[B][C] = 0$: no edge between $B$ and $C$

**Graph (star with center $A$):**

```
  B
   \
    A
   /
  C
```

Or equivalently:
```
B — A — C
```

$\square$

### Problem 7: Incidence Matrix for Star

Given the graph:

```
   M
  / \
 N   O
```

Edges: $e_1 = \{M,N\}, e_2 = \{M,O\}$

**Task:** Draw the incidence matrix.

**Solution:**

$$I = \begin{bmatrix}
  & e_1 & e_2 \\
M &  1  &  1  \\
N &  1  &  0  \\
O &  0  &  1
\end{bmatrix}$$

$\square$

### Problem 8: Graph from Adjacency List

Given adjacency list:

```
A : [B, C]
B : [A]
C : [A]
```

**Task:** Draw the graph.

**Solution:**

From the list:
- $A$ connects to $B, C$
- $B$ connects to $A$ (symmetric)
- $C$ connects to $A$ (symmetric)

**Graph:**

```
B — A — C
```

This is a path $P_3$ or equivalently a star $S_2$. $\square$

### Problem 9: Adjacency Matrix for Disconnected Graph

Given the graph:

```
1 — 2     3 — 4
```

Edges: $\{1,2\}, \{3,4\}$ (two components)

**Task:** Write the adjacency matrix.

**Solution:**

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}
  & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 \\
1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
2 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
3 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\
4 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

Notice the block diagonal structure reflecting two components. $\square$

### Problem 10: Adjacency List for Directed Graph

Given the directed graph:

```
U → V ← W
   ↓
   X
```

Edges: $(U,V), (W,V), (V,X)$

**Task:** Write the adjacency list.

**Solution:**

- $U: [V]$ (out-neighbors of $U$)
- $V: [X]$
- $W: [V]$
- $X: []$ (no outgoing edges)

For directed graphs, the list contains only out-neighbors. $\square$

### Problem 11: Incidence Matrix for Complex Graph

Given the graph:

```
   K
 /   \
L     M
 \   /
   N
```

Edges: $e_1 = \{K,L\}, e_2 = \{K,M\}, e_3 = \{L,N\}, e_4 = \{M,N\}$

**Task:** Create the incidence matrix.

**Solution:**

$$I = \begin{bmatrix}
  & e_1 & e_2 & e_3 & e_4 \\
K &  1  &  1  &  0  &  0  \\
L &  1  &  0  &  1  &  0  \\
M &  0  &  1  &  0  &  1  \\
N &  0  &  0  &  1  &  1
\end{bmatrix}$$

$\square$

### Problem 12: Graph from Incidence Matrix

Given incidence matrix:

$$I = \begin{bmatrix}
    & e_1 & e_2 & e_3 \\
V_1 &  1  &  0  &  0  \\
V_2 &  1  &  1  &  0  \\
V_3 &  0  &  1  &  1  \\
V_4 &  0  &  0  &  1
\end{bmatrix}$$

**Task:** Draw the graph and identify edges.

**Solution:**

**Edge identification:**
- $e_1$: incident to $V_1, V_2$ → edge $\{V_1, V_2\}$
- $e_2$: incident to $V_2, V_3$ → edge $\{V_2, V_3\}$
- $e_3$: incident to $V_3, V_4$ → edge $\{V_3, V_4\}$

**Graph (path):**

```
V1 — V2 — V3 — V4
```

$\square$

### Problem 13: Multiple Representations for Path Graph

Given the graph:

```
A — B — C — D
```

**Task:** Write both adjacency matrix and adjacency list.

**Solution:**

**(a) Adjacency Matrix:**

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}
  & A & B & C & D \\
A & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
B & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
C & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\
D & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

**(b) Adjacency List:**
- $A: [B]$
- $B: [A, C]$
- $C: [B, D]$
- $D: [C]$

$\square$

### Problem 14: Degree Calculation from Adjacency List

Given the graph:

```
S — T — U
|       |
R ——————
```

**Task:** Identify the degree of each vertex using the adjacency list.

**Solution:**

**Adjacency List:**
- $S: [T, R]$ → $\deg(S) = 2$
- $T: [S, U]$ → $\deg(T) = 2$
- $U: [T, R]$ → $\deg(U) = 2$
- $R: [S, U]$ → $\deg(R) = 2$

**All vertices have degree 2** (this is the cycle $C_4$). $\square$

### Problem 15: All Three Representations

Given the graph:

```
E — F
|   |
H — G
```

**Task:** Convert to all three representations.

**Solution:**

**(a) Adjacency Matrix:**

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}
  & E & F & G & H \\
E & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\
F & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
G & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\
H & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

**(b) Adjacency List:**
- $E: [F, H]$
- $F: [E, G]$
- $G: [F, H]$
- $H: [E, G]$

**(c) Incidence Matrix:**

Edges: $e_1 = \{E,F\}, e_2 = \{F,G\}, e_3 = \{G,H\}, e_4 = \{H,E\}$

$$I = \begin{bmatrix}
  & e_1 & e_2 & e_3 & e_4 \\
E &  1  &  0  &  0  &  1  \\
F &  1  &  1  &  0  &  0  \\
G &  0  &  1  &  1  &  0  \\
H &  0  &  0  &  1  &  1
\end{bmatrix}$$

$\square$

## Summary

This assignment covered:
- **Adjacency matrix:** $\Theta(n^2)$ space, $O(1)$ edge lookup
- **Adjacency list:** $\Theta(n+m)$ space, efficient neighbor iteration
- **Incidence matrix:** $\Theta(nm)$ space, useful for edge-based operations
- Conversion between representations
- Directed vs. undirected graph representations

Understanding these trade-offs is critical for selecting the right representation for different algorithms and applications.
